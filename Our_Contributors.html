Our Partnership Stories

Discover how our contributors have helped us navigate our journey

Ocean Wise

$2,000 in funding

As an Ocean Wise Youth Partner, we align perfectly with their mission of ocean conservation and education. Their support has enabled us to develop technologies that directly address marine biofouling issues, reducing both toxic microplastic release and invasive species transfer.

Through our partnership, we've presented at networking dinners, sharing our innovations with the broader conservation community and amplifying our impact.

Shared Goals:

  • Ocean Conservation
  • Environmental Education
  • Sustainable Technology

SFU Engineering Science

$8,000 in total support

The Faculty of Engineering Science at SFU has been instrumental in our growth, providing not just financial backing but also academic guidance and resources. Their investment helps us offer hands-on experience to engineering students while showcasing the innovative spirit of SFU.

Our recent presentations at high schools highlight this mutual commitment to inspiring the next generation of engineers and environmental innovators.

Shared Goals:

  • Engineering Education
  • Student Development
  • Technical Innovation

Seymour Marine

Essential components & mentorship

Seymour Marine's contribution extends beyond hardware - their industry expertise and mentorship have been invaluable in guiding our technical development. By providing specialized parts and technical knowledge, they've helped us test and refine our designs to industry standards.

Our Project ZIMA complements their existing products, creating a synergistic relationship that advances marine technology as a whole.

Shared Goals:

  • Marine Technology Innovation
  • Product Integration
  • Industry Standards Development

Chang Institute

Co-op funding & professional development

The Chang Institute's support through co-op funding allowed us to dedicate full-time talent to Subvision's development, accelerating our progress significantly. Their mentorship has opened doors to industry connections and opportunities that would otherwise be inaccessible.

This partnership exemplifies how academic institutions can directly enable real-world innovation and professional growth.

Shared Goals:

  • Professional Development
  • Research Application
  • Innovation Acceleration

MATE ROV

$1,500-2,000 travel bursary

MATE ROV's support enabled our team to compete on an international stage, testing our innovations against global standards. The competition provided invaluable real-world testing scenarios and connected us with the broader marine technology community.

The experience and recognition gained through this partnership have been catalysts for our ongoing project development and team growth.

Shared Goals:

  • Technical Competition
  • Educational Excellence
  • International Collaboration
